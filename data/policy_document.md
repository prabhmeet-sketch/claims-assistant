# ClaimsCo Reimbursement Policy (v3.2, effective 2026-04-01)

## 1. Auto-Approval Cap
Claims of $5,000 or less that meet all documentation requirements may be
auto-approved by the system without human review.

Claims above $5,000 REQUIRE human authorization regardless of confidence
or evidence strength. This is a policy control, not a confidence threshold.

## 2. Extended Protection Plan (Electronics)
Devices purchased with the Extended Protection Plan are covered for
accidental damage, including damage in transit, for 24 months from
purchase date. No deductible applies for shipping-related damage.

## 3. Property Damage (Water, Fire, Weather)
Claims for property damage require:
  - An incident date within the policy period
  - A repair invoice or damage estimate
  - Photos are recommended but not mandatory

## 4. Travel Cancellation — Hotel Bookings
Free cancellation is permitted only if cancellation occurs at least
48 hours before scheduled check-in. Cancellations within the 48-hour
window are NOT covered by this policy and are the customer's
responsibility, unless the property itself issued a refund.

NOTE (known internal inconsistency, v2.9 legacy FAQ still circulating
in some support scripts): an older, deprecated FAQ document told
customers "cancellations are free with no time limit." That FAQ was
retired in v3.0 (2026-02-01) and is no longer valid. Support agents
have been inconsistently trained; some customers may still repeat the
old guidance. Any claim referencing "no time limit" cancellation should
be treated as a conflicting-source case, not resolved automatically in
either direction.

## 5. Required Escalations (Authorization Controls)
The following ALWAYS require human review, regardless of confidence:
  - Claimed amount exceeds $5,000
  - Customer explicitly requests a human reviewer
  - Any fraud, legal, or safety indicator is present

## 6. Required Escalations (Uncertainty Controls)
The following require human review because evidence is incomplete or
conflicting:
  - Two policy sources disagree and precedence rules don't resolve it
  - Mandatory documentation is missing
  - The tool that would confirm coverage is unavailable or failed
